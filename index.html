About Me

Hi, my name is Ravi Kothari, and I am a sophomore computer science major at the University of Maryland- College Park. During my time as a computer science major and by spending some of my free time learning, I have acquired several skills that have allowed me to succeed within the program. I have developed a true passion for the computer science field, and I hope to use these skills in the future as a software developer.

In addition to my work within the computer science field, I am also a volunteer EMT (Emergency Medical Technician). I started my volunteer EMT career after my junior year of high school, when I took the EMT-B course and became a New Jersey State Certified EMT. I currently volunteer at Princeton First Aid and Rescue Squad in Princeton, NJ and Branchville Volunteer Fire Company in College Park, MD. Over the past three years, I have risen from the ranks of a junior, probitionary member to a senior, active member. I have also recently started driving the ambulance in an emergency setting. Through my experiences as an EMT, I have grown as a person and I truley understood the idea of community service.

If you would like to find out more about me, please do not hesitate to contact me.
